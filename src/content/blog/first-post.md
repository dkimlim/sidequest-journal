There's something about deviating from the plan — just slightly — that opens up a texture the direct path doesn't have. You notice the odd shop. You end up in a conversation you weren't expecting. The neighborhood reveals itself at a different angle.

I've been thinking about this as a metaphor for how I work. The most interesting things I've made came from sidequests: the project that wasn't the main project, the question asked almost parenthetically, the tool built for fun that turned out to be the actual thing.

> The detour is often the destination.

This journal is an attempt to document those moments before I tidy them into something presentable. Raw notes, unfinished thoughts, links I can't stop thinking about.
